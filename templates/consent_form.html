Research Consent Form

Please read the consent form carefully:

Study Team

Students at Simon Fraser University studying perception of Contempt, Anger and Disgust (CAD) emotions in different cultures, towards the development of software which can better express or perceive non-verbal communication across cultures. We also want to investigate how individualistic scores of each culture affects CAD expressions in that culture. We would like you to help us understand which visual social signals and emotions are well recognized by the majority of people in this study.

Task Details

You are invited to take part in our study that helps to classify negative emotions of Contempt, Anger and Disgust (CAD) in three different cultures of North American, Persian and Filipino. We are looking for participants to label short clips, and select the expressions that are seen in each clip.

Voluntary Participation and Right to Withdraw

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time without no negative consequences or any penalty for doing so. If you choose to enter the study and then decide to withdraw at a later time, it will not be possible to remove the annotations you have already completed, as it will have been stored anonymously. Since you are given a code, it is possible for you to retrieve your personal data to be deleted in case of withdrawal.

Procedure

If you say “Accept,” is how we will do the study:


  • The study will take place through an online web application, and you will be asked to complete it by filling out the forms.
  • First, you have to register and enter general information about yourself (e.g. language, background culture, etc.) You will be given a code, that you must save it for yourself. This code is not recoverable if you forget it.
  • Then you will be asked to login, and after successful login you will be redirected to annotation page in order to begin labelling short videos (each video is 1 to 5 seconds).
  • Each culture has 40 to 45 clips. You will be rewarded a 5$ Amazon gift card for annotating each 25% of the clips (20$ in total).
  • You may withdraw at any point of annotation by clicking on withdraw link and confirming your code. Your reward will be calculated based on your progress.

The estimated time for annotating each clip is 2 to 3 minutes, and roughly 1.5 hours in total. You do not have to finish it in a sitting.

Risks/Discomforts

We do not think there is anything in this study that could harm you, and there are no foreseeable risks to you in participating. If you decide to take part in this research study and are unable to complete it, you may end the study by clicking on withdraw link, confirming your code and informing the researchers.

Benefits

Although it may not directly benefit you, this study may benefit society by improving the ability of intelligent agents that interact with humans in emotion recognition, which could be embedded into future technologies and provide better user experience.

Moreover, you will be paid (in Amazon gift cards) upon completion or withdrawl from the task.

Confidentiality

There is no identifying information in this study. Your random generated code is completely anonymous to conceal your identity. Some general information will be stored in a secure database about you with the code. The authentication mechanism purpose in this project is only to keep track of your progress. The main study findings will be published in academic journal articles and present at academic conferences. The data obtained from this study will be kept indefinitely on a web-based repository (e.g. SFU Vault or Radar) to be preserved for future use in open access initiatives.

Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

We set inclusion criteria as all workers over the age of 19 years old, with a computer, that are related and familiar with one of three cultures: North American, Filipino or Persian, and are located in Canada.

You will be excluded from this study if you do not know any of English, Tagalog or Persian languages.
